Research

I study surfaces in hyperbolic 3-manifolds. Specifically, I have been working on the following:

Limits of increasingly geodesic quasifuchsian surfaces in a closed hyperbolic 3-manifold (In preparation)

Abstract: Let \(M\) be a closed hyperbolic 3-manifold. Let \(\nu_{\Gr M}\) denote the probability volume (Haar) measure of the 2-plane Grassmannian \(\Gr M\) of \(M\) and let \(\nu_T\) denote the area measure on \(\Gr M\) of an immersed closed totally geodesic surface \(T\se M\) We say a sequence of \(\pi_1\)-injective maps \(f_i:S_i\to M\) of surfaces \(S_i\) is asymptotically Fuchsian if \(f_i\) is \(K_i\)-quasifuchsian with \(K_i\to 1\) as \(i\to \infty\). We show that the set of weak-* limits of the probability area measures of \(f_i(S_i)\), where \(f_i:S_i\to M\) are increasingly Fuchsian minimal or pleated maps of closed connected surfaces \(S_i\), consists of all convex combinations of \(\nu_{\Gr M}\) and the \(\nu_T\).
